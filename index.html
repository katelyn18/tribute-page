The Life of Alan Turing

  • 23 June 1912 - Born in London, England
  • 1922 - 1926 - Went to Hazelhurst Preparatory School
  • 1926 - Went to Sherborne School
  • 1931 - 1934 - Studied at King's College, Cambridge as an undergraduate
  • 1935 - Elected as a Fellow of King's College
  • 1936 - Published "On Computable Numbers, with an Application to the Entscheidungsproblem"
  • 1936 - 1938 - Studied at Princeton University under Church
  • 1938 - Obtained a PhD at Princeton (Department of Mathematics); worked part-time with the GC&CS
  • 1939 - Reported to Bletchley Park
  • 1942 - devised the Turingery technique
  • 1945 - 1947 - Worked on the design of ACE in Hampton, London
  • 7 June 1954 - Died in Cheshire England, age 41
